About Alireza Sadeghi

Hello and welcome to my website!

Alireza Sadeghi is currently a research associate at the University of Minnesota's ECE department working in Signal Processing in Networking and Communication Group (SPiNCOM) under supervision of Prof. Georgios B. Giannakis. He holds B.Sc. degree (Hons.) from the Iran University of Science and Technology and an M.Sc. degree from the University of Tehran, both in Electrical and Computer Engineering. Since 2016, he has been dedicated to pursuing a Ph.D. at the University of Minnesota, with a research focus spanning machine learning, optimization, and signal processing in networking. His academic excellence is recognized through an ADC Fellowship and student travel awards from the IEEE Communications Society and the National Science Foundation. In addition to academia, Alireza has played a pivotal role as a Data Scientist at 3M, leveraging GPT-2 Generative Model for Advanced Data Retention Strategies. His contributions include developing a DialoGPT model for doctor-patient dialogues and implementing Controlled Text Generation using BART-based summarization models. His commitment to transparency is evident in his work on Explainable Text Generation, where he designed methods to discern evidence for generated summaries, showcasing the integration of academic rigor and real-world impact. Furthermore, Alireza made significant contributions during his internship at Microsoft Research, focusing on Data Science and Optimization for the energy grid. His research, working on Deep Reinforcement Learning and Optimization techniques to seamlessly integrate renewable energy into data center operations. His use of advanced methodologies, including dual decomposition and deep Q-network-based algorithms, aimed to optimize the efficiency and sustainability of data center functioning. Hello and welcome to my website! I am Alireza Sadeghi a Postdoctoral associate at the Electrical & Computer engineering department at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, and a member of the Signal Processing in Networking and Communications (SPiNCOM) group under the supervision of Professor Georgios B. Giannakis. My research interests involve Signal Processing, Statistical Learning, Machine Learning, and Optimization with their application in applications in designing reliable, scalable, and efficient cyber-physical systems including next-generation wireless and power networks. Particularly I am interested in designing robust and scalable supervised, reinforce- ment, semi-supervised, and federated learning algorithms. Specifically, I am currently working on Robust Meta-Learning, Self-supervised Learning, and Representation Learning with Weak supervision. I am currently looking for self-motivated Ph.D. students interested in statistical signal processing and learning, weak supervision and crowdsourcing, and self-supervised learning. Please send me an email if you are interested! Previously I was a Data-Scientist working in 3M Corporation.

Education and Awards

Dr. Sadeghi received the Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from University of Minnesota in 2021. He further received the M.Sc. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Tehran in 2014, and the B.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering from the Iran University of Science and Technology in 2011, both with the highest rank. He is the recipient of the Best Ph.D. Thesis award from both the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering at Imperial College London during the academic year 2018-2019, as well as the IEEE Information Theory Chapter of UK and Ireland in the year 2019. Also, his paper titled "Federated learning over wireless fading channels" received the IEEE Communications Society Young Author Best Paper Award in the year 2022.
